Working At CIBC

Introduction

Hi everyone! My name is Thanush and I am currently enrolled and completing a Bachelor of Computing in Computer Science (co-op) at the University Of Guelph. As you are reading this I have completed 2 years in school and am about to complete my first co-op work term. My first work term was spent at CIBC (Canadian Imperial Bank Of Commerce) as a Technical Systems Analyst with the Market Data Support team at CIBC Capital Markets.

While working at CIBC, I had the opportunity to gain valuable experience by completing a variety of purposeful projects with the help of a supportive team. Throughout this report, I will discuss my experience here at the bank and how this company has positively impacted my career.


Information About The Employer

CIBC also known as Canadian Imperial Bank Of Commerce is one of the top 5 banks in Canada. Supporting over ​​14 million personal banking, business, public sector and institutional clients in Canada, the U.S. and around the world. The office is based off of 161 Bay Street on the 4th floor, which is actually named TD Canada Trust Tower. Within this building consisted of many floors belonging to CIBC, including the trade floor. The area of computing science that is related to this company is IT troubleshooting as well as software development.

Job Description And Projects

An overview of my job consisted of many things. At the start of my co-op I had the opportunity to help with the onboarding of summer students within Capital Markets. This consisted of installing trading software applications onto users' machines, applications like Bloomberg. There were over 150 incoming summer students, a big project with a short deadline. This project consisted of skills I had to learn on the job, skills involving installing as administrator, navigating through user’s computers and troubleshooting any errors or issues. Other projects I got to work on varied from day to day, our team had a helpdesk number that users on the trade floor would be able to contact if an issue were to occur. Issues involving any of the trading softwares they use. For example, a user isn’t able to pull data from Bloomberg into excel. This would result in me troubleshooting and connecting to their machine in order to resolve the issue. I also had the opportunity to tackle a coding project provided by my manager Matthew Cheung. The project was to utilize the Bloomberg B-pipe API (blpapi) to request and pull real time data regarding different currencies. This project was coded in java, and involved many skills I learned during my time at school. Skills such as API requests, coding in java, and testing and debugging my code.

Conclusion

Overall this co-op experience has been a pivotal stepping stone in my career development. It has allowed me to experience what working in the computer science field is like and what it has to offer. It has also opened my eyes to the many career paths within Computer Science. I have learned what it’s like to work collaboratively with a team, and to be able to perform under pressure. I have also learned many technical skills, including network and systems administrator skills. Skills that I believe will benefit me in the future. As I continue the next 4 months as a Technical Systems Analyst with the Market Data Support team, I am eager to continue to grow and learn within this role.
